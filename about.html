Visual Artist & Digital Generalist

the background

bridging tactile & virtual

My practice explores the translation of memory into physical and digital environments. I work at the intersection of traditions and contemporary computation, using light as a primary medium to bridge the gap between the tactile and the virtual.

I respond to the perceived coldness of digital media by introducing sensibilities drawn from traditional practices like painting and sculpture. I view technology not as a separate entity, but as an extension of human and natural patterns—a medium that requires the same care, stewardship, and spirit as the physical world.

By housing digital processes within physical metaphors, such as fabric or water, I create spaces where abstract data feels lived-in and atmospheric. My goal is to create immersive environments where the digital becomes a site for reflection, connection, and ethereal experience.

Biography

Kiana Rezaee is a Montreal-based Iranian visual artist and digital generalist. Her interdisciplinary practice spans interactive installation, sculpture, and computational media, exploring how technology can reflect and reimagine complex human experiences. Originally trained in traditional painting and sculpture, her work has evolved into the creation of immersive environments that merge physical materiality with responsive digital systems.
